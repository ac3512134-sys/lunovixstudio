Origin Story

Why Lunovix Studio Built This

Pomodo Focus was engineered because modern productivity tools are fundamentally broken. They're either bloated with features nobody uses, locked behind subscription walls, or secretly optimized to keep you inside the tool rather than doing actual work. Lunovix Studio was founded on a single principle: the best productivity tool is one that disappears and lets you work.

Version 5.0 introduces the fully-realized vision — a three-panel neural workspace with Luna AI on the left, your focus engine in the center, and your soundscape and analytics on the right. Everything is accessible without navigation. Everything is live.

How It Works

  1. Sign In or Create Profile

    Your profile powers the leaderboard, persists your stats, and personalizes Luna. Use password "demo" for instant access.

  2. Set Your Soundscape

    Pick a sound from the right panel — Quantum Rain, Lo-Fi Matrix, White Noise, Bio-Forest, Soft Piano, or the Interstellar organ theme. Sounds generate live, no downloads needed.

  3. Consult Luna AI

    The left panel is your always-on AI co-pilot. Ask for schedules, motivation, time plans, or Pomodoro guidance. Luna responds with personalized, actionable advice.

  4. Engage Your Session

    Hit ENGAGE on the center timer. Your analytics update in real-time in the right panel. The bell chimes when your cycle completes.

  5. Track Progress

    Your 7-day output map, streak, total minutes, and session count all live in the right analytics panel — always visible, always current.

Interstellar Sound

The Interstellar Piano Theme

The Interstellar soundscape is inspired by Hans Zimmer's iconic score for the 2014 Christopher Nolan film. The score is built around a massive pipe organ — an instrument chosen by Zimmer to represent the vast, inhuman scale of space and time. The defining characteristic is its slow, layered, building quality: notes sustain and overlap, creating an atmosphere of profound depth and forward momentum.

Our Web Audio API synthesis recreates this using layered oscillators tuned to Zimmer's harmonic language (D minor, F major), with long attack and decay envelopes to simulate organ pipe resonance, plus a reverb convolution to create the vast spatial depth of the film's sound. The result is a slow, evolving soundscape that builds over time — perfect for sustained deep work sessions where you need to feel the weight and importance of your work.

FAQ
Is the Interstellar sound actually from the film?
No — it is an original synthesis inspired by Hans Zimmer's compositional language and the emotional texture of the score. It is not a reproduction of any copyrighted material. All audio is generated procedurally using Web Audio API oscillators.
How does the leaderboard work?
The leaderboard ranks all browser-stored accounts by total focus minutes. It includes demo competitors so it's never empty. In a single-device setup, all accounts compete together. A shared cloud leaderboard is on the Lunovix roadmap.
Is my data private?
Yes. All data — login credentials, stats, tasks, settings — is stored exclusively in your browser's localStorage. Nothing is sent to any server. Your data never leaves your device.
Is this free?
Completely free, forever. No subscriptions, paywalls, ads, or accounts required beyond the local login. Lunovix Studio believes great tools should be accessible to everyone.
